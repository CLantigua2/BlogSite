A long way up

Apr 2, 2018

   Back around October 2014 I made a huge life decision, and I wasn't really sure if it was the right choice to make or not. At this point, I was nearing 10 years in the Marines working logistics and distribution. I loved what I did and enjoyed being a Marine but I felt it was time try new things. This however, didn't go well for about a year. I had this idea that my military experience would amount to more than minimum wage (I was wrong haha). As my savings started draining I decided to take an airplane mechanic entry level job at around $14 an hour. After a year I was promoted to quality inspector (my current job).

   I began looking into giving college another shot as the mundane feeling began to set in from work. I started talking to a friend of mine that I've known for the last 15 years and he tells me he's doing web development. To be perfectly honest I had no clue what Web Development even consisted of at the time. I never really gave it much thought as to how websites would randomly show up, who worked them, and what it took to put one together. I started looking up information, anything I could find from articles, to videos and job market statistics. If you ever want to feel overwhelmed, Google search what it takes to become a Front End Developer. You'll see a huge list of things that "EVERY" developer MUST know. Once my freaking out settled down, I asked my friend where I should start. He's been asked this question many times before, gladly gives up the information and would watch people fall off before the first month is over.

   He pointed me to FreeCodeCamp and told me to do the entire front end course and if I was still around after that he would tell me what to do next. I started back in November 3rd and got a majority of the front end completed, I enjoyed the challenges and constant learning. I could only do it for a few hours while at home as I still had a full time job so I looked up other resourses to train my brain to think like a dev. I was really into podcasts while at work so I looked up a few this subject, turns out there are some pretty cool ones that you can check, here are a few of my favorites.

   Start Here PodCast - Dain Miller

   Syntax - Wes Bos and Scott Tolinki

   Full Stack JavaScript Podcast - Dario Cantatore

  Simple Programmer - John Sonmez

  I hightly recommend them if you get a chance. I find that listening to conversations about programming, even if you don't understand what they're talking about can help you pick up on keywords and technologies. I looked up a few things they would mention that FCC didn't talk about like editors, libraries and frameworks. On the 4th Month I decided to take a deep dive into JavaScript, I honestly was not getting the stuff that FCC was teaching me on JQuery and felt like they were just going through random motions but did not cover any eccentials that I needed to do anything on my own. I was looking through different sources for JavaScript learning starting with a Udemy course called The Web Developer Boot Camp by Colt Steele. Possibly the coolest name in Web Development I think. This course was good but I felt like it was a bit too fast pace or advanced for me. I realized this when I decide to embark on my own project and sat there with a blank text editor not know how to even start a function. I honestly almost gave up and felt like a lot of my effort was going to go down the drain until I decided to try out Team TreeHouse as a last effort. I'll post next time to let you know how it has gone so far for me along with a few things I learned a long the way that might help a beginner not feel so lost.
