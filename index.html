About Me


"If opportunity doesn't knock, build a door."
Milton Berle

What's up, Im Carlos, and like a lot of people first starting out, I am trying to make the transition from my current job to a career in web development (big change!). I have been self learning since September 2017 through multiple online sources. Because of my full time job and family, my regular schedule has been approximately 2 to 4 hours of learning on weekdays to 8 to 12 hours on weekends. If this sounds like you and you want to hear about a similar experience, or just looking to find out what worked for me and what didn't check out my blog.

What's this blog about?

Through this blog I hope to document my progress on a daily or weekly basis. I also plan to talk about materials that are helping me (sites, videos, books, articles, ect) through my journey. I'd also like to talk about cool frame works and library that I find along the way.

Some of my favorite references

Treehouse

has been my #1 go to site for learning. They cover a huge array of topics and even split them all into tracks so you don't have to go on a hunt for what should be next.

FreeCodeCamp

is amazing free learning. The site runs fully on donations also has a great set of tracks to follow as well as projects and challenges.

TraversyMedia

has a lot of more advanced topics such as librarys and frameworks. Almost every time I search a topic on youtube, I find that he's already made a video on it.

Web Design with HTML, CSS, JavaScript and JQuery Set

These books are very easy to read and good to have as a reference. With so much to learn it is good to look at something with simple virbage and examples.

StackOverFlow

This forum has saved countless lives from oblivion and continues to be a place where devs help each other out. Just make sure you explain what you need and tag your questions well or you could be waiting forever to get help.
